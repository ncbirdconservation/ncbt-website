Description

Although small at 11 acres, Wilson Botanical Gardens provides a great location to get your birding fix in the Northwestern Coastal Plain. The probability of seeing significant birds is highest during Spring, Fall and Winter. During the growing season, birders and non-birders alike can enjoy the variety of garden types on display as the Gardens were designed to showcase includes an extensive tree collection, and native plant exhibits. While the Gardens also feature a Children's Secret Garden, it is currently under construction.

Species of Interest (Full Bird List)

Brown Thrasher, Northern Mockingbird, Blue Jay, Eastern Bluebird, Great Blue Heron, Red-tailed Hawk, Northern Cardinal

Habitat

oak forest & mixed hardwood/pine

Special Features/Concerns

The site hosts a bird garden. Visitors can call ahead to receive a tour of the gardens. Pets are not permitted.

Access and Parking

Hours: Dawn until Dusk, 365 days a year. Bathroom facilities open Monday-Friday 8 AM -5 PM and closed holidays. The parking area has 140 spaces and is handicapped accessible.

Directions

From HWY 264 take HWY 301 N. Turn left onto Goldsboro Street. The gardens will be on the left before reaching Ward Blvd.
